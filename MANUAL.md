# XQUEST 2
## Sega Genesis / Mega Drive
### by Mark Mackey — Sega Genesis port

---

## INTRODUCTION

XQuest 2 is a horribly addictive arcade game. Gems, mines, bonuses, and lots of things to wantonly blow up — they're all here, now playable on your Sega Genesis.

---

## THE STORY

You're a jolly little ship dingus which shoots all around a rather abstract landscape collecting little gem thingies, while avoiding mines scattered around. Boring, you say? Well, we kept the Super Kill-o-Zapper, and added LOTS of things to blow up. Happy?

---

## CONTROLS

### D-Pad
Move your ship in **8 directions**. Your ship always faces the direction you are moving, and fires in that direction.

| Direction | Ship faces |
|---|---|
| Right | → |
| Left | ← |
| Up | ↑ |
| Down | ↓ |
| Up + Right | ↗ |
| Up + Left | ↖ |
| Down + Right | ↘ |
| Down + Left | ↙ |

### Buttons

| Button | Action |
|---|---|
| **A** or **C** | Fire Super Kill-o-Zapper |
| **B** | Activate SmartBomb |
| **START** | Pause / Resume |

While paused, press **A** to quit to the title screen.

---

## HOW TO PLAY

Your objective is to **collect all the gems** on each level. When every gem has been collected, a **gate** opens at the top of the screen — fly through it to advance to the next level.

Complete levels quickly to earn a **time bonus**, which boosts your score and your chances of making the Hall of Fame.

### The HUD

The top of the screen shows:

```
SCORE:00012500   L03   SHIPS:3   BOMBS:2
```

| Element | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **SCORE** | Your current score |
| **L##** | Current level number |
| **SHIPS** | Remaining lives |
| **BOMBS** | Remaining SmartBombs |

### Scoring

| Action | Points |
|---|---|
| Collect a gem | 50 |
| Destroy an enemy | 100 – 500 (varies by type) |
| Time bonus (fast level clear) | Up to 5,000 |

### Extra Lives

You earn an extra ship every **15,000 points** at lower levels. The threshold increases gradually at higher levels.

---

## HAZARDS

### Mines

Red mines are scattered around the playfield. Touch one and your ship is instantly **Smashed into Small Pieces of Putrid Debris™**. Shoot them to clear a path.

### Enemies

Enemies appear at the start of every level, and more types appear as you progress. Do not underestimate any of them.

| Enemy | Description |
|---|---|
| **Grunger** | Slow and stupid hired muscle. Fairly harmless on its own. |
| **Zippo** | Same as a Grunger, except for the slow bit. |
| **Zinger** | Fires little teeny bullets in all directions. |
| **Vince** | Nearly invulnerable. Avoid rather than engage. |
| **Miner** | Lays mines right where you don't want them. |
| **Meeby** | Big, annoying, tough. Need more be said? |
| **Retaliator** | You shoot them, they shoot right back. Fair? Hardly. |
| **Terrier** | Seems harmless until it catches your scent. Then it homes in. |
| **Doinger** | The longer it's alive, the more dangerous it becomes. Kill early. |
| **Snipe** | An excellent marksman just *waiting* for a clear shot at you. |
| **Tribbler** | Very fragile and very friendly — until you shoot it. |
| **Buckshot** | Fires LOTS of little teeny bullets everywhere. Terminate on sight. |
| **Cluster** | Slow, harmless, quite cute really. Until you shoot it. |
| **Sticktight** | Harder to shake off than a heat-seeking missile, and twice as deadly. |
| **Repulsor** | You may not have much choice about running. Run anyway. |

---

## SMARTBOMBS

If things get desperate, activate a SmartBomb with **Button B**. It destroys every enemy currently on screen in one glorious blast. You start each game with **3 SmartBombs**.

SmartBombs are precious — use them wisely. You can collect additional SmartBombs by picking up PowerCharges (see below) or finding them in the playfield.

---

## POWERCHARGES

Occasionally a **PowerCharge** will drift across the screen. Collect it immediately — they evaporate quickly and cannot be shot without destroying them (don't do that).

Collecting a PowerCharge grants one of several random bonuses:

- An extra SmartBomb
- A score bonus (+1,000 points)
- An extra life
- Temporary invincibility
- A brief burst of rapid fire

The effect is random, but PowerCharges are **nearly always beneficial**. If you shoot one by accident, the recommended course of action is to pause the game, find a solid brick wall, and bang your head on it repeatedly.

---

## DIFFICULTY LEVELS

Select your difficulty from the Options screen before starting a game. Each level has a **separate Hall of Fame**, so you can't cheat your way to glory on Wimp difficulty.

| Difficulty | Description |
|---|---|
| **Wimp** | For beginners and very small children. |
| **Timid** | You bounce off the walls instead of dying. |
| **Average** | The standard experience. |
| **Tricky** | Faster enemies and more of them. |
| **Inhuman** | Lightning reflexes and nerves of steel required. |

---

## TWO PLAYERS

Select **Two Players** from the Options screen to play cooperatively. Both players share the same screen. Player 1 uses Controller 1; Player 2 uses Controller 2. Controls are identical for both players.

The game ends when both players have lost all their lives.

---

## HALL OF FAME

If you finish a game with a high enough score, you will be invited to enter the **Hall of Fame**. Type your name with the D-Pad and confirm with A. Your glory is saved to SRAM and will persist between sessions.

Each difficulty level maintains its own independent Hall of Fame — a Wimp score does not compete against an Inhuman score.

---

## TIPS

- **Kill Miners first.** Every mine they lay is a hazard you'll have to dodge or shoot later.
- **Don't chase Snipes.** They want a clear shot. Keep moving unpredictably.
- **Shoot Clusters carefully.** They split into more dangerous fragments when hit. Save a SmartBomb if you're surrounded by them.
- **Collect gems near enemies last.** Clear the enemies first, then grab the gems safely.
- **Watch the gate position.** It opens at the top-centre of the screen. Plan your exit route before collecting the final gem.
- **PowerCharges won't wait.** If you see one, go get it immediately.
- **SmartBombs clear the screen.** If you're overwhelmed, use one — that's what they're for.
